Anti-Ragging

  • Ragging in any form is strictly prohibited and is a punishable offense under law.
  • The institute follows a zero-tolerance policy towards ragging both inside and outside the campus.
  • Students found guilty of ragging may face suspension, expulsion, or legal action as per UGC guidelines.
  • All hostels are under CCTV surveillance and regular rounds are conducted to monitor student activity.
  • First-year students are allotted separate floors/blocks to ensure their safety and comfort.
  • An Anti-Ragging Committee and Squads are in place to handle complaints and take swift action.
  • Helpline numbers and email IDs for reporting ragging incidents are available 24/7.
  • All students are required to submit an anti-ragging undertaking at the time of admission.
  • Awareness programs and workshops are conducted to educate students about the consequences of ragging.
  • Victims or witnesses of ragging can report anonymously without fear of retaliation.
